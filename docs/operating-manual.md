# Operating Manual

> Practical day-to-day usage guide for Personal and Org nodes.

---

## 0. What This System Is

This is a **cybernetic regulation system**, not a productivity or planning system.

Its job is **not** to decide what you should achieve.

Its job is to:

- keep you viable
- prevent collapse
- allow learning without coercion
- preserve agency and optionality
- create conditions where ambition can compound
- disappear when nothing is wrong

This is not a system for wanting less. It is a system for pursuing what you want without self-destruction.

If the system is "busy," something is off.

## 7. Baseline vs Intervention

### Baseline State (Success)

- No Episodes open
- You act freely
- No justification required
- No tracking

Baseline is not stasis. It is the ground from which ambitious moves become possible. You don't launch from exhaustion â€” you launch from rest.

### Out of Baseline

- optimization pressure
- feeling trapped
- needing certainty

Response:

- Open **minimal** Stabilize Episodes
- Reduce authority
- Shorten time horizons

## 8. Continuity Work

**Continuity work is allowed background activity with zero authority.**

It:

- keeps skills warm
- preserves identity continuity
- does NOT aim to fix anything

Examples:

- touching music without release pressure
- refactoring existing org material
- organizing archives
- documentation

**Test:** If you stopped for two weeks and nothing breaks â†’ it qualifies.

Continuity work:

- does NOT go on whiteboards as Episodes
- may be noted briefly as "allowed background activity"

## 10. Crisis Protocol

### True crisis = loss of basic functioning or safety.

In crisis:

- **Suspend all Explore Episodes**
- **Allow only Stabilize actions**
- **Block irreversible decisions**
- **Seek external support immediately**

This is not system failure. This is correct escalation.

If you are in the U.S., you can call or text **988** for immediate support.
